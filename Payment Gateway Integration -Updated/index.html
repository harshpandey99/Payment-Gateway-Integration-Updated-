wash your place

Clean and disinfect surfaces frequently especially those which are regularly touched, such as door handles, faucets and phone screens.

maintain distance

don't touch face

When you touch your face, the germs on your hands can take up residence in your mucous membranes and cause infections, including COVID-19 infections.

wash your hand

Frequent and proper hand hygiene is one of the most important measures that can be used to prevent infection with the COVID-19 virus.

use napkin

Cover your mouth and nose with a flexed elbow or tissue when coughing and sneezing. And throw away the used tissue immediately in a bin with a lid and to wash your hands.

wear a mask

Masks and face coverings can prevent the wearer from transmitting the COVID-19 virus to others and may provide some protection to the wearer.

take precautions against covid-19

things you should DO

wash your hand

To prevent the spread of germs during the pandemic, you should wash your hands with soap and water for at least 20 seconds or use a hand sanitizer with at least 60% alcohol to clean hands.

always wear a mask

Masks are a key measure to suppress transmission and save lives. Depending on the type, masks can be used for either protection of healthy persons or to prevent onward transmission.

stay home during fever

Stay home and self-isolate even with minor symptoms such as cough, headache, mild fever, until you recover. If you have a fever, cough and difficulty breathing, seek medical attention.

things you should NOT DO

avoid close contact with animals

SARS-CoV-2 spreads primarily through human-to-human transmission, but there is evidence of transmission between humans and animals. Several animals like mink, dogs, domestic cats, lions, tigers and raccoon dogs have tested positive for SARS-CoV-2 after contact with infected humans.

avoid close contact with peoples

Maintain at least 1 meter (3 feet) distance between yourself and other people, particularly those who are coughing, sneezing and have a fever.Wear cloth face coverings in public settings where other social distancing measures are difficult to maintain.

avoid crowded area

The risks of getting COVID-19 are higher in crowded and inadequately ventilated spaces where infected people spend long periods of time together in close proximity. These environments are where the virus appears to spread by respiratory droplets or aerosols more efficiently, so taking precautions is even more important.
